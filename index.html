Football

Since I can remember, I have done a variety of sports growing up: football, track, baseball, and basketball. However, the one sport that I truly invested most of my time and dedication in was football. There, I found a community and environment unlike any other, where I learned what true discipline, hard work, and teamwork are, with a group of 100+ all striving to win a game of football. In high school, a coach once told me, "Football is a privilege, and it has given me what I have today, so I owe football. Play the game to give back for all that it has given you."

This ideal stuck with me throughout high school and led me to the decision to pursue the sport inter-collegiately. Now, I play for the Pomona-Pitzer Varsity football team as a DB! Currently, I am in the off-season, preparing for my final year of football and my final year of competitive football ever!

Taiko (Japanese Drums)

I remember playing an arcade game called "Taijutsu no Taiko" and always had a fascination with Japanese drums. Once I heard about the "Shogo Taiko" club, I went to the first practice of the semester and enjoyed the sound, movement, and collaboration that came with it. Through this, I was able to explore aspects of my culture I had never thought of, as well as having a great group of members where we coordinated to play the Taiko drums through various songs. Now, I hold the position of Treasurer and will have my last few performances before graduating in December!
